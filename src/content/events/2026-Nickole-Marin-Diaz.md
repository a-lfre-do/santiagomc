We are pleased to invite you to the course **“Basic Neuroanatomy,”** which will be taught by **Nickole Marín-Díaz** (Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez, Chile) during the month of April at **Universidad Alberto Hurtado**.

Neuroanatomy is a fundamental tool for contemporary philosophical research. The study of the brain and the central nervous system allows us to address, with greater precision, classical questions about the relationship between mind and brain, as well as problems related to perception, memory, and consciousness. In this sense, understanding the organization and function of different brain structures not only provides an empirical basis for discussing philosophical theories, but also opens up new questions about conscious experience and the limits of neurobiological explanations.

The course includes **7 units** and will be held in **4 in-person sessions**, from **10:00 to 12:30** (2 clock hours plus a 30-minute break), on the following dates:
